    Early Life

    Furthermore, her mother was a famous violinist and her father was a bass player and managed a TV station. She was the granddaughter of an architect, Howard Dwight Smith, who designed the Ohio (“Horseshoe”) Stadium at Ohio State University. 

    Beverly D’Angelo belongs to a Mixed ethnicity and her nationality is American. D’Angelo was born with the astronomical sign of Scorpio. She was a single girl child of her parents.

    D’Angelo grew up with her three brothers, Jeff, Tim, and Tony. Talking more about her childhood, it is quite obvious to be inclined toward music as she has musical genes inherited from her mother.

    Beverly D’Angelo was passionate about rock and jazz music among other genres. But, in her teens, she shifted her focus from music to acting.

    Similarly, she was an ambitious artist since her teenage years and her inclination towards animation shows was seen. Particularly, she was fond of the animation made by Hannah Barbara Studios.

    Education

    Regarding her educational background, most of her schooling was completed in Italy. Later, she left for the USA, and Beverly D’Angelo wished to pursue a career in animating and music got an end in midway.

    Beverly D’Angelo – Professional life

    Music career

    During the initial stage of her career, Beverly D’Angelo worked as an animator/cartoonist at Hanna-Barbera Productions before moving to Canada to pursue a rock singing career. To keep the wolf from the door, D’Angelo also worked as a session vocalist and left no place to sing be its coffeehouses and bars.

    Furthermore, she was invited to join up with rockabilly legend, Ronnie Hawkins. Soon, she joined the band, ‘The Hawks’ which is now popular as ‘The Band’.

    Her acting career started when she toured Canada as Ophelia in “Kronborg: 1582”, a rock musical version of Shakespeare’s “Hamlet”. Fortunately, D’Angelo’s performance could not go out of Colleen Dewhurst’s sight.

    Gradually,  Gower Champion got into the mix and the show was completely revamped, becoming the rock musical “Rockabye Hamlet”, which made its way to Broadway in 1976.

    Acting career

    After this show, D’Angelo never stepped on the stage but did star alongside Ed Harris in the 1995 off-Broadway production of Sam Shepherd’s “Simpatico”. In 1976, she appeared in the first three episodes of a television miniseries titled ‘Captains and the Kings’.

    This series was lauded by the audience which brought confidence in her. Not only confidence but this miniseries also got her introduced to Woody Allen, who presented her with a small yet key role in 1977’s ‘Annie Hall’.

    D’Angelo appeared as ‘Actress’ in ‘Rob’s T.V. Show’ while Woody Allen was in the lead role. The same year, she also played the role of Shelley in First Love and ‘Sandra’ in ‘The Sentinel’.

    Her acting career took off and even Hollywood could not help from admire her. In 1987, she appeared in ‘Every Which Way but Loose’ and ‘Hair’.

    However, her breakthrough was the role of ‘Patsy Cline’ in ‘Coal Miner’s Daughter’. It was a biopic and based on true events, and Angelo was praised worldwide for her brave portrayal of the meaty role.

    Likewise, she also sang in this film for her character which eventually nominated her for a Golden Globe Award for ‘Best Actress in a Supporting Role category.

    Similarly, there was no looking back for her, she ruled Hollywood by the portrayal of some roles from prostitutes to rich brats, widows to troubled mothers in her films.

    Her presence in various roles in movies like  ‘Paternity’, ‘Finders Keepers’, ‘The Pope Must Diet’, and ‘Crazysitter’.Her movie ‘A Streetcar Named Desire’ brought her a few main roles in films like ‘Slow Burn’ and ‘Sweet Temptation’.
