    Bears Issued Statement Over Brian Urlacher Instagram Story

    Brian Urlacher's recent Instagram story has attained some controversies around. Urlacher comparedNBA players boycotting playoff games to Brett Favre's Monday Night Football performance one day after his father died.

    09 May 2024, 00:00

    Brian Urlacher's recent Instagram story has attained some controversies around. Urlacher compared NBA players boycotting playoff games to Brett Favre's Monday Night Football performance one day after his father died. 

    After the Bucks-led boycott of Wednesday night's NBA games (26 August 2020) and the eventual postponement of all NBA games on 27 August 2020, Urlacher came with a message on his Instagram story. He wrote:

    "Brett Favre played the MNF game the day his dad died, threw 4 TD's in the first half, and was a legend for playing in the face of adversity. NBA players boycott the playoffs because a dude reaching for a knife wanted a felony sexual assault warrant, was shot by police."

    However, things never went as Urlacher thought. He was quick to garner the critics and controversies about his message on Instagram.

    (©: Instagram/Brian Urlacher)

    Even Urlacher's team did not support him. Chicago Bears has already said to distance themselves from the Hall of Fame linebacker on Thursday. The Bears issued a statement on 27 August 2020 which read, 'The social media posts in no way reflect the values or opinions of the Chicago Bears organization.'

    Similarly, the former Bears player Matt Forte responded to the Instagram message of Brian Urlacher.

    (©: Twitter/Matt Forte)

    Furthermore, Urlacher's post created a rivalry between two schools of thought. Some praised him for sharing his opinion while others called for Urlacher to be 'canceled' from the team.

    Brian Urlacher's Married Life

    Brian Urlacher, age 42, has been married twice throughout his life. Back in a decade, he was married to his first wife named Laurie Urlacher. The Urlacher couple first met in 1999 during a football game and later hooked up.

    A year in June 2000, the former lovebirds exchanged their wedding vows. The couple went on welcoming two kids, namely, Pamela and Riley, in the course of their married life. However, their marriage did not last longer than four years, and eventually, they got divorced in 2004.

    Brian Urlacher and his second wife, Jenifer Frost (©: Sports Gossip)

    Later, in 2016, he tied a knot with his second wife, Jennifer Frost, a former contestant on America's Next Top Model. The pair are together since then.

    Besides married life, Brian Urlacher has been several love affairs. He dated Tyna Robertson, with whom he shares a son named Kennedy. Similarly, he was in a relationship with a model and actress Jenny McCarthy for four months from April 2012 to August 2012.

    Brian Urlacher's Stats And Net Worth

    Brian Urlacher, who stands at the height of 6 feet and 4 inches tall, played his professional career from Chicago Bears for twelve years until his retirement. The Bears drafted him in the 2000 NFL Draft. In the same year, the American football linebacker signed a five years contract with the team for eight million dollars with a five and a half million dollar signing bonus.

     

    Brian Urlacher in NFL (©: The Spun)

    His career stats suggest that he played 182 games, where he made 1,037 solo tackles, 316 assists, 348 touchdowns. He got retired in 2013 after announcing his retirement through Twitter on 22 May 2013.

    Through his football career, he has garnered the net worth of $16 million and also cashes a salary of $5.6 million per year. He is the winner of the NFL Defensive Player of the Year Award for the 2005 season. Similarly, he got named the second most overrated player in the league behind Terrell Owens by the 2006 Sports Illustrated poll of 361 NFL players.
