    The New Zealand Cricket Coach is James Edward Charles Franklin

    James Edward Charles Franklin, born in New Zealand on November 7, 1980, is a name linked with cricket greatness. This New Zealander shone on the international scene as a left-arm medium-fast bowler and middle-order batsman. His efforts to New Zealand cricket have left an indelible mark, and he resigned from the game in 2018.

    James Franklin’s Notable Achievements

    James Franklin’s coaching career is littered with game-changing accomplishments. He made history by becoming the first African-American head coach of a major sport at Vanderbilt. Furthermore, he became the Southeastern Conference’s (SEC) third African-American head football coach. Three bowl game appearances throughout his career at Vanderbilt attest to his coaching abilities. At Penn State, he achieved the summit by winning the Big Ten Championship and being named Big Ten Coach of the Year in the same year. These achievements cement his position in American football coaching history.

    James Franklin Media Coverage

    James Franklin’s effect goes beyond the field. He has been a media fixture, appearing in notable magazines such as ESPN, Sports Illustrated, and The New York Times. Franklin has received great acclaim for his teaching talents and steadfast dedication to multiculturalism. In 2017, The Root honored him as one of the 100 Most Influential African Americans in Sports.
