    Biography of Raymond Peter O’Keefe Jr.

    Raymond Peter O’Keefe Jr. was born on April 24, 1955, in Mount Vernon, New York State, USA. He is a well-known film and television actor, recognized for his roles as Danny Noonan in “Caddyshack” (1980) and as Barry Grissom in “Michael Clayton” (2007), among other appearances. O’Keefe’s career took off in the mid-1970s.

    Michael O’Keefe has built his net worth of $2 million through a successful career in the entertainment industry, appearing in over 100 film and TV titles.

    Michael O’Keefe Net Worth $2 Million

    Michael, the eldest of seven children, was born to Raymond Peter O’Keefe and Stephanie. He comes from Irish ancestry and was raised in Larchmont, New York, where he attended the American Academy of Dramatic Arts and later, New York University.

    His journey in the entertainment industry began in 1970 with a role in a Colgate commercial, and he then joined Joseph Papp’s Public Theater in New York City. O’Keefe made his on-screen debut in the minor role in the TV series “The Texas Wheelers” in 1974. Throughout the ‘70s, he made several minor TV appearances, before landing the role of basketball player Ben Meechum in the drama “The Great Santini” (1979), which earned him an Oscar Award nomination.

    In 1980, he gained recognition for his role as a golfer in the comedy “Caddyshack”. He continued to make significant appearances in various films as the ’80s progressed, further boosting his career and net worth, including roles in “Savage Islands” (1983), “The Slugger’s Wife” (1985), and the Oscar Award-nominated drama “Ironweed” (1987).

    Transitioning into the new decade, O’Keefe continued to feature in successful projects, including the romantic comedy “Nina Takes a Lover” (1994) and the Oscar Award-nominated drama “Ghosts of Mississippi” (1996). He further solidified his place in Hollywood with roles in “The Glass House” (2001) and “The Hot Chick” (2002).

    In recent years, O’Keefe has been active in television, appearing in series such as “Homeland” (2014), “Masters of Sex” (2015-2016), and “Sneaky Pete” (2017), all contributing to his wealth.

    In his personal life, Michael has been married to Emily Donahoe since 2011, and they have one child. Previously, he was married to musician Bonnie Raitt from 1991 to 2000.
